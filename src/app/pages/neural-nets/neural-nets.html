Neural Net Creatures

Creatures with small neural networks evolve to survive — eating food, reproducing, and competing.

[P] pause    [+/-] speed    [WASD] camera    click creature to inspect

How it works

Each creature is controlled by a small feed-forward neural network. Inputs include nearby food distance, nearby creature distance, current energy level, and the creature's facing angle. Outputs drive forward thrust and turning.

When a creature accumulates enough energy it reproduces, passing its weights to an offspring with small random mutations. Over many generations, effective behaviours emerge without any explicit fitness function.

Tech

Written in Go and compiled to WebAssembly using Ebiten, a 2D game engine. The neural network, creature physics, and world simulation all run at 60 TPS inside a single WASM binary with no JavaScript simulation logic.
